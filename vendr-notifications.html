Helping Customers Stay in the Know

Vendr's notification system had grown organically across email and Slack without a coherent strategy — templates were redundant, content was inconsistent, and users had no centralized way to manage their preferences. This project overhauled the entire system, introduced in-app notifications for the first time, and established the standards that six product teams now build on.

Background

For a product built around keeping buyers informed — renewals, approvals, tasks, contract deadlines — broken notifications meant broken trust. Six product teams were each handling notifications independently, which meant no consistency, no shared standards, and no visibility into what was being sent across the product.

Users were receiving inconsistent, sometimes redundant messages across email and Slack with no way to manage their preferences in one place. There was no in-app notification experience at all. And with no catalog of what was being sent, the team had no way to know the scale of the problem before this project began.

The problem

Notifications had grown organically across email and Slack without a coherent strategy. Templates were redundant, content was inconsistent, and there was no centralized way for users to manage their preferences — and no in-app notification experience at all.

Because six product teams were each solving notifications on their own, the same inconsistencies kept reappearing. Without shared standards or visibility into what was being sent, fixing individual messages would never have been enough — the system itself needed a foundation.

The approach

Before designing anything, my team and I worked to understand the full system — auditing every existing notification, studying how leading products handle them, and aligning the six product teams on a shared set of patterns.

What we shipped

A connected overhaul of the notification experience — unified designs across email and Slack, an in-app inbox introduced for the first time, and the content standards that keep it all consistent. Here's what shipped.

Impact

Built to be democratized. By establishing shared standards and migrating to Courier, we gave all six product teams the foundation to iterate on notifications independently — without creating new inconsistencies.

Outcomes

19% increase in click rate for task-based emails
74% of users engaged with the new notification settings on launch
11 redundant templates deprecated — reducing noise and maintenance overhead
6 product teams aligned on shared notification standards they now build on independently

What I learned

The most valuable thing this project produced wasn't the in-app notification experience — it was the standards. By democratizing the notification system and giving each product team clear guidelines, we made it possible for the whole org to iterate on notifications without creating new inconsistencies.

That's the difference between solving a problem and building infrastructure that prevents the problem from recurring. The 74% settings engagement on launch was gratifying — but the fact that six teams can now ship notifications independently, consistently, is the outcome that scales.
